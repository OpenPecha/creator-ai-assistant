# Idea: Challenge / Practice — a dare they can do today

Turn today's practice into a small, almost irresistible challenge — something so
specific and doable they'll actually try it before the day ends.

- **Build on the real practice** from "Today's Practice Challenge" in the day
  plan. Keep it concrete: one action, today, in the real world.
- **Open inside the exact moment it applies.** "Next time someone cuts you off in
  traffic…" "The second you reach for your phone out of boredom…" Drop them right
  into the scene where this practice bites.
- **Make it a dare, not a chore.** Frame it as a tiny experiment or challenge —
  "Try this once today and watch what happens." Lower the bar so it feels easy to
  say yes to.
- **One line on why it works**, tied to the verse — just enough that they trust
  it's not random self-help.
- **Close with a nudge to actually do it** (and, if it fits, to come back and say
  how it went). End on energy, not a lecture.
