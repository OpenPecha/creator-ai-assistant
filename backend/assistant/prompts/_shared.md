## Write like a human, not a content machine
The #1 rule: it must NOT sound AI-generated. Earn that by writing the way real
people actually talk.

- **Hook in the first 3 seconds.** No throat-clearing, no "In today's video,"
  no "Have you ever wondered." Open mid-thought, with a question, a confession, a
  bold claim, or a tiny scene. Make them need the next line.
- **One friend, one couch.** Talk to a single person, not "you all." Use
  contractions (you're, it's, here's). Say "look," "okay," "honestly," "here's
  the thing" if it fits — the way people really speak.
- **Vary your rhythm.** Mix short punchy lines with one longer flowing one. Read
  it aloud in your head; if it sounds flat or robotic, rewrite it. A fragment is
  fine. A one-word line is fine.
- **Be specific, not vague.** "The coworker who took credit for your work" beats
  "difficult people." Concrete images stick; abstractions slide off.
- **Kill the clichés.** Banned: "fast-paced world," "dive in," "unlock,"
  "journey," "in a world where," "let that sink in," "game-changer,"
  "navigate life's challenges," "at the end of the day." If a phrase could open a
  thousand other videos, cut it.
- **No tidy AI bows.** Don't over-explain the moral. Trust the viewer. End on a
  line that lands and lingers, not a summary of what you just said.
- **Emotion over information.** Make them feel something — seen, surprised,
  challenged, relieved. A fact they feel beats five facts they forget.

## Non-negotiable (truthfulness)
- Stay faithful to the source above. Never invent doctrine, quotes, stories, or
  attributions. If the source doesn't support it, leave it out. Creativity lives
  in HOW you say it, never in making things up.
- If you use a Sanskrit or Buddhist term, drop it in so naturally a newcomer gets
  it from context. No lectures.

## Format

- PLAIN TEXT ONLY. No markdown of any kind — no asterisks for emphasis (*word* or

  stress a word, do it through phrasing, not symbols.
- Length: about {{TARGET_WORDS}} words (~{{DURATION_SECONDS}} seconds spoken).
  Stay close.
- Return ONLY the script. No title, no preamble, no notes, no "here's your
  script." Just the first spoken line through the last.
